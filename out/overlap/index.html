META
How overlap is computed

Three numbers per pair

  • 1. Shared count. The primary ranking. How many tickers appear in both managers’ top-holdings lists. A higher count means deeper consensus.
  • 2. Jaccard overlap. Shared / union. A correction for portfolio breadth — two managers with 20 holdings each sharing 5 is a 14% Jaccard; two managers with 10 holdings each sharing 5 is 33%. Penalizes the “big book wins by default” effect.
  • 3. Joint weight. For every shared ticker, take the smaller of the two managers’ position weights and sum. This tells you how much of both portfolios the shared book actually represents — the depth of the shared conviction, not just the breadth.
